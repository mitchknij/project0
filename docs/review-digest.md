# VERDICTS

## CLAUDE.md
CLAUDE.md passes its own arithmetic — 13/15 iron rules, 4/5 CAPS lines, kit core+footer 40/55 lines, all matching README's claims — and the routing-table concept, TRIGGER-line contract, and hard-stop format are genuinely well engineered. But it fails its own two hardest standards where they matter most: (1) single-sourcing is systematically broken — seven of thirteen iron rules restate doc content rather than point to it, and the twin copies have already drifted (word lists, forbidden-phrase sets, status formats), exactly the decay F7 exists to prevent; and (2) the route-then-announce contract has three real seams: a blocker where the 'first Edit of this session' row plus the footer's cache invalidation leaves no compliant path to ever re-read CODE.md after compaction, an act-before-read loophole created by the batching iron rule, and an unbounded '(cached) — obey from memory' branch that a mid-tier model will use to skip checklist items invisibly. Secondary but real: judgment words ('large', 'will outlive this sitting', 'unsure') survive in the always-loaded routing rows in violation of the kit's own F2/F14, and two of the four hard stops have weakened '-> instead:' clauses (a Windows-only replacement command in a portable kit; 'confirm' without 'with the user'). All fixes are small, local text edits; the blocker (row 2 wording) should land first since it silently disarms the entire CODE.md discipline for the post-compaction half of every long session — precisely the sessions the kit targets.

## PLAN.md
PLAN.md is structurally sound against the kit's format contract (correct divider, second-person reference headers, stable IDs, mostly numeric thresholds, well under the 120-line cap) and P4's escape hatch ("Cannot fill FILES yet? investigate, then post it") shows the authors anticipated the investigate-before-plan problem — but the doc has six substantive weaknesses. The two most consequential are execution-order problems the assignment specifically probed: the walk instruction demands one line per item "in your reply" while P3/P5 require tool runs and P4 defers itself to first-Edit, inviting fabricated compliance lines at task start; and the scope-escalation brake in the reference section triggers on "2x your TASK block estimate" when the TASK block records no estimate, so the kit's main anti-scope-creep rule can never fire deterministically. Two single-source (F7) violations — P2 restating a CLAUDE.md iron rule and the twin-definition section restating CODE.md C3, both with wording drift already visible — plus a judgment-state header ("You are tempted...") guarding the YAGNI/dependency rules, and a missing docs/STATE.md bootstrap that PLAN's own PLAN-CHANGE procedure depends on, round out the majors. Remaining findings are token-list and phrasing polish. Nothing is an outright contradiction that would produce wrong code, so no blockers; fix the two ordering/computability majors first, since they gut the doc's enforcement exactly at the moment it matters.

## CODE.md
CODE.md is dense, in budget (99 lines vs the ~120 target — not too long to read fully), and most checklist items are genuinely checkable, but it has one blocker and a cluster of majors concentrated exactly where the assignment pointed. The lifecycle split is mostly right except C14, a pre-deletion gate filed under "After each edit" whose own text says "greps first", and C11, whose language-independent corruption check (git diff) is trapped inside a Python-only scope, leaving all non-Python edits with no after-edit gate at all. REFERENCE SWEEP is neither unambiguous nor complete: step 1's "OLD identifier" is undefined for most of C12's triggers (signature/return-shape/enum changes where the name is unchanged), and the "paste every hit, disposition every hit, do not proceed" mandate collides head-on with EFFICIENCY.md E11/E8, which forbid scrolling >50-hit results and require delegating find-all-usages sweeps — a live inter-doc contradiction with no reconciliation. The trap tables are findable by category label, but C7's index doesn't match the headers: the Familiar-API table has no trigger token (and its confidence traps never hit the "unsure? probe" hatch, so it's effectively dead), "range endpoints" has no row, and "negated conditions" doesn't name its "Boolean logic" header. Rather than cutting length, move the test-shares-literals section to VERIFY.md (its trigger occurs in the debug/verify flow, and nothing routes to it here) and add the one-line C15 hook to make the rewrite section reachable; several dead-end Nevers (C5, C9) also need their mandated "-> instead:" exits, and the C4/SESSION.md CONSTRAINT CHECK format has already drifted between its two homes.

## DEBUG.md
DEBUG.md is structurally one of the kit's stronger docs — event-phrased trigger, ID'd checklist, paste verbs, correct divider, accurate C3/STATE.md cross-references — but it has one genuine contradiction and several rationalization holes exactly where a mid-tier model would exploit them. The blocker: D4's hard cap of 'full suite at most twice per task' collides with D8's mandatory post-fix suite run the moment a confirmation run fails and a second fix lands, producing a procedural deadlock in the single most common debugging scenario. The escalation ladder's levels are self-declared and uncountable at L3+ (investigation actions have no defined 'failure'), so it needs its floors tied to the checkable ATTEMPT count in the ledger; D1's 'cannot reproduce -> stop' is a zero-effort exit with no symptom-match requirement; D5's flake handling is keyed to whether the model typed a phrase, which is trivially gamed; and the red-flag table misses the four phrases stuck models actually emit ('different approach', 'pre-existing', 'add a sleep', 'caching issue'). Three single-source violations (D10 vs CLAUDE.md hard stops with already-divergent lists, D2 vs SESSION.md's environment block, the test-expectation clause vs V10) show drift starting. All fixes are local rewrites; no structural redesign needed.

## VERIFY.md
VERIFY.md is structurally sound (correct trigger restatement, ID'd checklist, divider, accurate pointers to docs/STATE.md sections that SESSION.md actually defines) and its evidence-table and forbidden-phrase machinery are the strongest anti-hallucination devices in the kit — but the enforcement core has three genuine breaks: the echo protocol's FAIL rule literally contradicts its own N/A option (and N/A needs no reason, making it the abusable escape the assignment predicted), the status vocabulary contradicts itself across V7/line-25/line-36 with four spellings of one token, and V8's `git diff --stat` is vacuous once changes are staged — silently defeating the scope audit in the most common commit flow. The pattern-completion risk is real: nothing ties a quoted 'output line' to an actual tool result in the transcript, and V2's verbatim example line is a ready-made fabrication template; one added sentence ('the quoted line must appear in a tool result in THIS turn') closes most of it. Coverage of the V-items is otherwise good (freshness, tests, artifacts, monorepos, scope, endpoints, triviality) with only mild overlaps (V1 vs the evidence table, V2 vs V3 on skips) and one rule conflict (V4's 'never completion evidence' vs typecheck-goal tasks); contradictions with DEBUG.md are absent in substance but V10 duplicates DEBUG's expectation-change rule wholesale, and the Forbidden-phrases section duplicates a CLAUDE.md iron rule with visible drift — both single-source violations. Fix the three blockers and the tool-result-anchoring sentence and this doc does its job; leave them and a mid-tier model can pass the whole checklist without running a single command.

## EFFICIENCY.md
EFFICIENCY.md is one of the kit's stronger docs — the read-less/read-enough architecture is sound, most rules carry real numbers and literal commands, and E2 correctly defers to CODE.md C1 (I found no conflict there). But it has one genuine contradiction and a cluster of drift/portability defects. The blocker: E15's 10-line final-answer cap collides head-on with VERIFY.md's mandatory 12-line V-echo in the same completion turn, handing a mid-tier model a rule-sanctioned reason to skip verification evidence. The majors: E3's absolute 'never Read after your own Edit' contradicts the iron rule's re-Read-after-failed-Edit procedure and never points to E4, whose event list is the only definition of 'scope changed' — so the read-less/read-enough pairing fails precisely at the isolated-citation moment it was designed for; the doc's line-2 trigger drifts from the routing table ('context is filling' is unroutable judgment prose, 'large file' is unnumbered); E10's flagship redirect `cmd *> build.log` silently glob-corrupts in bash; E16's Bash-tool/POSIX mapping is wrong on the kit's own primary (PowerShell-shelled) machine; and E14 duplicates a CLAUDE.md iron rule with an already-diverged tripwire word list, exposing an unresolved kit-wide question about whether iron-rule/doc duplication is sanctioned under F7. The ID jumps themselves are correct F12 behavior and fine for citation — they just need a one-clause note so a future editor doesn't renumber and a reader doesn't hunt for missing IDs. Fix the blocker and the five majors and this doc holds its weight.

## SESSION.md
SESSION.md's content is faithful to the research digest and its numbers are good (80 lines, 10/15 messages, >200 lines, depth 2), but it is the least format-compliant doc in the kit and has two structural holes that break it exactly when it matters. The remaining issues are single-source paraphrase drift (ATTEMPT format in three variants, Constraints row duplicating the CLAUDE.md iron rule, line 8 duplicating the footer re-arm), short-path 'see DEBUG.md' cross-references violating F10, a 'copy verbatim' template instruction that would paste placeholder meta-text into the live file, and a DECISION: keyword missing from README's audit grep list. MIGRATE.md is the strongest-conceived file in the kit — the transport-not-authorship principle, line-accounting table, and echo-style Verify lines are exactly right — but execution-walking it exposes one genuinely destructive bug and a cluster of self-contradictions a mid-tier model must rationalize around. ## fact-check
The CODE.md trap tables are factually very solid: of ~40 concrete claims, I probe-verified nearly all against Python 3.14.4 and Node 24.14.1 and they check out exactly as written — [10,9,1].sort() -> [1,10,9]; ['1','7','11'].map(parseInt) -> [1, NaN, 3]; Array(3).map holes; new Date('2024-01-15') = UTC midnight vs new Date(2024,0,15) = local (getDate() -> 14 vs 15 on this UTC-7 machine); replace() first-only; reused /g lastIndex statefulness; '$$' -> '$'; structuredClone preserving Dates/undefined vs JSON.stringify dropping them and nulling NaN/Infinity; toFixed -> string; 2**53 === 2**53+1; typeof null; -1%5 = 4 (Python) vs -1 (JS); ((x%n)+n)%n = 4; re.match vs fullmatch (including the '$ matches before \\n' case); str.split() vs split(' '); int-interning (100 is -> True, 1000 is -> False); mutable-default persistence; [[0]*n]*m aliasing; lambda i=i pinning; var/let closures; utcnow() naive (+DeprecationWarning) and naive<aware TypeError; epoch digit counts (time.time() 10 digits, Date.now() 13); py_compile exit 1 on syntax error; the inspect.signature one-liner; and the knowledge-verified JWT-exp-seconds, currentTimeMillis, Go Unix()/pre-1.22 loop capture, and Rust/Go/C/Java truncating % are all correct.
